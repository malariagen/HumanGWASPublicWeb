[@Variantcatalog]

The [@variantcatalog] displays basic properties for a set of single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) on the genome and the allele frequencies for the populations sampled in this dataset.

Single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) are positions in the genome where a single nucleotide varies between individuals. They are identified by aligning sequence reads of different samples to each other or to a reference genome, and are an important measure of genetic variation between individuals and across populations.

Actions

  • Change the query in the left panel to return a subset of [@snps] that have certain properties.
    • Restrict to a specific region on a chromosome
    • Restrict to a region spanned by a specific gene by clicking on the Find Gene button
    • Restrict to [@snps] with a specific frequency pattern over the populations
    NOTE: after each change in the query, press the Update query results to update the content of the table.
